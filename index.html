Why only IDLE and URLLC_ACTIVE in evaluation?
The framework works so effectively that URLLC waiting time never exceeds 30% of Lmax in these episodes (threshold for urllc_warning = 50%). WARNING, CRITICAL and VIOLATION states only appear during training, where 441/800 episodes had critical events. This is a positive result — the safety layers prevent deadline escalation entirely.

💡 Why does only 1 of 7 fallback steps show a penalty?
The fallback policy fires preemptively — it activates when uncertainty is high AND a URLLC user's waiting time exceeds 70% of the deadline, but before the deadline is actually missed. In 6 of 7 cases the fallback successfully served the URLLC user in time, preventing any violation. Only 1 step had an actual deadline breach (QoS penalty −500), meaning the packet had already exceeded Lmax before the decision could take effect.
Fallback triggers ≠ deadline violations. The framework is proactive, not reactive.
